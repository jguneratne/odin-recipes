Gluten Free Sesame Seed Crackers

Struggling to find a great gluten free cracker recipe? These sesame seed crackers are the perfect solution!

We all know how difficult it is to find a great gluten free cracker. It's even more difficult to find a great gluten free cracker recipe. I've done the hard work for you and tested out a couple of combos that make a great treat for those who struggle with celiac disease or gluten intolerance.

The ingredients can all be found in the health section of your local supermarket, or purchased in bulk online. Don't let the gluten free name fool you. No one will know the difference! In the list below, all ingredients were from gluten-free sources, even if not specified in the list.

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup Rolled Oats
  • 1/3 cup Flaxseed meal + 1/4 cup in reserve
  • 1/2 cup Sesame Seeds
  • 1/2 cup Flaxseeds (whole)
  • 1/4 cup Poppy Seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Sea Salt
  • 4 tsp chopped fresh Rosemary (more or less to taste)
  • 2 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 2 tbsp Lemon Juice
  • 1/2 - 1 cup Warm Water
  • 2 tbsp Honey

Steps

  1. Put the Rolled Oats into a food processor and grind to a rough flour. Transfer to a mixing bowl and add 1/3 cup Flaxseed meal, Sesame Seeds, whole Flaxseeds, Poppy Seeds, Sea Salt, and Rosemary.
  2. In a glass measuring cup, add 1 cup warm water and dissolve 2 tbsp honey and 3 tbsp lemon juice.
  3. Add 2 tbsp Olive Oil to the dry ingredients and mix together until it's incorporated into a crumbly mixture. Start adding the water, honey and lemon mixture to the dry ingredients until it just comes together into a sticky mixture, but not too wet. Start with 1/2 cup and add more water up to 1 cup if needed. It takes time for the Flaxseed meal to absorb water, so after adding 1/2 cup of water, wait 5-10 minutes to see how wet it is. If it's too dry, add more water. If it becomes too wet, you can add more Flaxseed meal, up to 1/4 cup.
  4. Preheat oven to 350. While it's heating, line a large sheet pan with parchment paper or a silicone mat and transfer mixture to the sheet. Use a second piece of parchment paper to spread the mixture thinly across the sheet pan. This mixture is easier to spread than the buckwheat mixture. If you get cracks or breaks you can squish them back together. If the mixture spreads unevenly to one side, cut it off and move it to an area with less mixture. Spread as evenly as possible.
  5. Put in oven and bake for 10 minutes. Remove from oven and reduce heat to 275. Score the mixture into 1-1.5" squares (roughly 60 crackers per batch). Return to oven and bake for at least 30 minutes. If the crackers are spread unevenly, some might be finished earlier than others. You can take those off the sheet and bake the rest until crisp.
